# Goals of Fall Prevention

Fall prevention isn't just about avoiding injury—it's about maintaining your independence, confidence, and quality of life.

## Primary Goals

### 1. Prevent Falls from Happening
The best outcome is never falling at all. This program focuses on:
- Identifying and reducing risk factors
- Strengthening your body
- Making your environment safer
- Managing health conditions that increase risk

### 2. Minimize Injury If a Fall Occurs
If you do fall, being physically strong and knowing how to fall safely can reduce injury severity:
- Strong bones are less likely to break
- Good muscle strength helps you catch yourself
- Knowing safe fall techniques can protect your head
- Quick access to help reduces complications

### 3. Maintain Independence
Fall prevention helps you:
- Continue living in your own home
- Stay active in the community
- Keep doing activities you enjoy
- Avoid institutional care

### 4. Preserve Quality of Life
By preventing falls, you can:
- Stay confident and fearless
- Remain socially engaged
- Continue hobbies and interests
- Maintain physical fitness

## Setting Personal Goals

Your fall prevention plan should include specific, achievable goals. Examples:

**Physical Goals**
- Walk 30 minutes daily without feeling unsteady
- Get up from a chair without using your hands
- Stand on one leg for 10 seconds

**Home Safety Goals**
- Install grab bars in the bathroom
- Improve lighting in hallways
- Remove all throw rugs

**Health Management Goals**
- Have vision checked annually
- Review medications with doctor
- Attend physical therapy sessions

## Measuring Success

Track your progress by noting:
- Improved balance or strength
- Feeling steadier when walking
- Greater confidence in daily activities
- Completion of home modifications
- No falls in the past month/year

## A Realistic Approach

### What Fall Prevention CAN Do
- Significantly reduce your risk
- Help you feel more confident
- Improve your overall health and fitness
- Give you practical strategies

### What Fall Prevention CANNOT Do
- Guarantee you'll never fall (some risk always exists)
- Reverse all effects of aging
- Work without your active participation

## Your Role in Prevention

Success requires:
- **Awareness**: Recognize your personal risk factors
- **Action**: Make recommended changes
- **Adherence**: Stick with exercises and modifications
- **Communication**: Talk openly with healthcare providers
- **Patience**: Improvements take time

## Working with Your Healthcare Team

Your goals should align with medical advice. Work with:
- Primary care physician
- Physical therapist
- Pharmacist
- Occupational therapist
- Vision and hearing specialists

## Long-Term Commitment

Fall prevention isn't a one-time project—it's an ongoing lifestyle:
- Continue exercises even after strength improves
- Maintain home safety as needs change
- Stay vigilant about new risk factors
- Adjust strategies as you age
