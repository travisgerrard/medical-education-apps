# Introduction

Now that you understand your fall risks, it's time to make your home safer. Home modifications are among the most effective fall prevention strategiesâ€”and many are simple and inexpensive.

## Why Focus on Home Safety?

**The facts:**
- **55% of falls** happen at home
- Most falls occur in familiar places, not unfamiliar ones
- Simple changes can reduce home fall risk by **30-50%**

## Your Home Assessment

Before making changes, walk through your home room by room:
- Look for hazards you've stopped noticing
- Get down to floor level to see trip hazards
- Test lighting at different times of day
- Ask family or friends for fresh perspectives

## The Pay off

Making your home safer allows you to:
- Move confidently through your space
- Stay independent longer
- Reduce fear and anxiety
- Prevent injuries
- Age in place successfully

## Categories of Home Modifications

### No-Cost Changes
- Removing clutter
- Rearranging furniture
- Improving habits (turning on lights)
- Storing items at accessible heights

### Low-Cost Changes ($0-$100)
- Non-slip mats
- Night lights
- Removing throw rugs
- Cord organizers
- Bright light bulbs

### Moderate-Cost Changes ($100-$500)
- Additional lighting fixtures
- Grab bars
- Handrails
- Raised toilet seats
- Shower benches

### Major Modifications ($500+)
- Stair lifts
- Walk-in showers
- Bathroom remodels
- Ramps

**Good news**: Most effective changes are free or low-cost.

## Getting Started

**Start with high-impact, low-cost changes:**
- Remove all throw rugs today
- Add nightlights tonight
- Clear walking paths this week

**Then tackle one room per week:**
- Week 1: Bathroom (highest risk)
- Week 2: Bedroom and hallways
- Week 3: Living spaces
- Week 4: Stairs

## Remember

Your home should support your independence, not threaten it. Even small changes make a meaningful difference in your safety and confidence.
