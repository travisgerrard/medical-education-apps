# Introduction

Physical strength and balance are your body's best defense against falls. This section focuses on exercises and activities that keep you steady and strong.

## Why Exercise for Fall Prevention?

**The evidence is clear:**
- Exercise is the **single most effective** fall prevention strategy
- Research shows **23-34% reduction** in fall rates (2019 Cochrane review)
- Benefits seen in as little as 6-8 weeks
- Works at any age or fitness level

## What Makes Exercise Effective?

### Builds Muscle Strength
- Stronger legs catch you when you stumble
- Stronger hips improve stability
- Core strength maintains upright posture

### Improves Balance
- Trains your body's balance systems
- Increases confidence in movement
- Helps you recover from stumbles

### Enhances Flexibility
- Greater range of motion
- Easier to avoid obstacles
- Reduces injury if you do fall

### Boosts Bone Strength
- Weight-bearing exercise strengthens bones
- Reduces fracture risk if you fall
- Slows osteoporosis progression

## Types of Fall-Prevention Exercise

### Balance Training (Most Important)
- Standing on one leg
- Tai Chi
- Yoga
- Walking heel-to-toe

### Strength Training
- Bodyweight exercises (squats, chair stands)
- Resistance bands
- Light weights
- Functional movements

### Flexibility and Stretching
- Maintains range of motion
- Reduces stiffness
- Improves posture

### Aerobic Exercise
- Walking
- Swimming
- Cycling
- Dancing

## How Much Exercise Do You Need?

**Minimum effective dose:**
- **Balance exercises**: 3+ times per week, 20-30 minutes
- **Strength training**: 2-3 times per week
- **Walking**: Most days, 20-30 minutes

**Important**: Consistency matters more than intensity

## Safety First

**Before starting:**
- Talk to your doctor if you have health concerns
- Start slowly and build gradually
- Use stable support when needed
- Stop if you feel pain, dizzy, or short of breath

## The Best Part

You don't need:
- A gym membership
- Special equipment (though some items help)
- Perfect health
- To be young or athletic

You just need:
- Commitment to regular practice
- Willingness to start small
- Patience for gradual improvement

## Remember

Falls aren't caused by aging—they're caused by weakness and poor balance. Both are modifiable through exercise at any age.

The exercises in this section are specifically chosen because they prevent falls. This isn't just "staying active"—it's targeted fall prevention training.
