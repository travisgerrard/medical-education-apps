# Introduction

Understanding your personal fall risk is the first step toward preventing falls. Everyone's risk is different, and identifying yours helps you focus on the most effective prevention strategies.

## Why Assess Your Risk?

Falls rarely happen by chance. They occur when multiple risk factors combine at the same moment. The good news: many risk factors can be reduced or eliminated.

## Categories of Fall Risk

### Internal Factors (Your Body)
- Muscle strength and balance
- Vision and hearing
- Chronic health conditions
- Medications you take

### External Factors (Your Environment)
- Home hazards (lighting, clutter, stairs)
- Footwear choices
- Weather and outdoor conditions

### Behavioral Factors (Your Choices)
- Activity level and exercise
- Alcohol use
- Rushing or multitasking
- Using assistive devices properly

## The Truth About Risk

**Important**: Having risk factors doesn't mean you will fall. It means you should take specific actions to protect yourself.

### Modifiable vs. Non-Modifiable Risks

**You CAN'T change:**
- Your age
- Past falls or injuries
- Some chronic conditions

**You CAN change:**
- Your strength and balance
- Your home environment
- How you manage medications
- Your footwear and habits
