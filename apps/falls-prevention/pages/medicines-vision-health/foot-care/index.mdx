# Foot Care and Footwear

Your feet are your foundation. Proper foot care and appropriate footwear are essential for balance and fall prevention.

## Why Feet Matter

**Your feet:**
- Provide your base of support
- Contain sensors that detect ground texture
- Must be strong and flexible for balance
- Absorb shock when walking

**Problems affect**: Balance, gait, pain, and fall risk.

## Common Foot Problems

### Peripheral Neuropathy
**What it is**: Nerve damage causing numbness

**Causes:**
- Diabetes (most common)
- Vitamin B12 deficiency
- Alcohol use
- Chemotherapy

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Can't feel ground
- Don't notice blisters/injuries
- Impaired balance
- Increased fall risk **2-3 times**

**Management:**
- Control blood sugar (if diabetic)
- B12 supplementation if deficient
- Daily foot inspections
- Protective footwear

### Bunions and Hammertoes
**Problems:**
- Painful deformities
- Affect shoe fit
- Alter gait
- Reduce balance

**Treatment:**
- Proper footwear
- Padding
- Orthotics
- Surgery if severe

### Plantar Fasciitis
**Symptoms:**
- Heel pain, especially morning
- Affects walking
- Causes limping

**Treatment:**
- Stretching exercises
- Supportive shoes
- Orthotics
- Ice

### Arthritis in Feet
**Impact:**
- Stiffness and pain
- Reduced flexibility
- Altered gait
- Balance problems

**Management:**
- Anti-inflammatory medications
- Exercise
- Proper footwear
- Assistive devices

### Calluses and Corns
**Why they matter:**
- Painful
- Change how you walk
- Can hide serious problems

**Prevention:**
- Proper-fitting shoes
- Regular foot care
- Moisturize skin

## Foot Care Basics

### Daily Inspection
**Check for:**
- Cuts or sores
- Blisters
- Redness or warmth
- Swelling
- Color changes

**Especially if you have:**
- Diabetes
- Peripheral neuropathy
- Poor circulation
- Vision problems

**Use mirror** if can't see bottom of feet.

### Toenail Care
**Proper trimming:**
- Cut straight across
- Not too short
- File sharp edges
- Never cut into corners

**Thick nails:**
- See podiatrist
- Special tools needed
- Risk of injury if you trim

### Skin Care
**Keep skin healthy:**
- Moisturize daily (not between toes)
- Dry thoroughly after bathing
- Check for fungal infections
- Treat athlete's foot promptly

## When to See a Podiatrist

**Regular visits if you have:**
- Diabetes
- Peripheral neuropathy
- Circulation problems
- Difficulty trimming nails
- Persistent foot pain

**Seek immediate care for:**
- Non-healing wounds  
- Signs of infection
- Sudden pain or swelling
- Color changes

## Footwear Essentials

### Proper Shoe Fit

**Characteristics of good shoes:**
- Fits snugly but not tight
- Room for toes (thumb width at end)
- Secure heel (doesn't slip)
- Supports arch
- Cushioned sole
- Non-slip bottom

**When to shop:**
- Late afternoon (feet swell during day)
- Bring socks you'll wear
- Try both shoes
- Walk around store

### Shoe Features for Fall Prevention

**Must-haves:**
- Flat or very low heel (if any heel, keep ≤1 inch)
- Wide, stable heel base (not narrow)
- Non-slip rubber sole  
- Secure fastening (laces, velcro, buckles)
- Good arch support
- Cushioned insole
- Lightweight

**Best examples**: Flat walking shoes, sneakers, athletic shoes

**Avoid:**
- High heels (2+ inches)
- Slip-on shoes without secure fit
- Smooth leather soles
- Platform shoes
- Backless shoes/slippers
- Worn-out shoes

### Indoor Footwear

**Best choices:**
- Athletic shoes
- Supportive slippers with backs and rubber soles
- Walking shoes

**Never:**
- Socks alone
- Bare feet
- Backless slippers
- Smooth-soled slippers

### Outdoor Footwear

**Walking/athletic shoes:**
- Good tread
- Ankle support
- Water-resistant
- Replace when worn

**Winter boots:**
- Non-slip soles
- Waterproof
- Good traction
- Easy to get on/off

**Ice cleats:**
- Attach to shoes
- Metal coils or spikes
- Dramatically improve traction
- $15-30, worth every penny

## Special Footwear Situations

### Diabetic Shoes
**Features:**
- Extra depth
- No internal seams
- Protective toe box
- Custom orthotics

**Medicare may cover** with prescription.

### Orthotics
**Custom inserts:**
- Support arches
- Cushion feet
- Correct gait abnormalities
- Reduce pain

**Getting orthotics:**
- Podiatrist evaluation
- Custom molded
- Insurance may cover

### Compression Socks
**Benefits:**
- Reduce swelling
- Improve circulation
- May help with balance

**Important:** Get proper fit, don't cut off circulation.

## Shoe Replacement

**Replace shoes when:**
- Soles worn smooth
- Heels worn unevenly
- Upper material damaged
- Lost cushioning
- Over 6-12 months old (even if look fine)

**Cost vs. safety:**
- Good shoes: $50-150
- Hip fracture treatment: $40,000+
- **Shoes are a bargain**

## Exercises for Foot Health

### Toe Curls
- Place towel on floor
- Use toes to scrunch toward you
- Strengthens foot muscles

### Ankle Circles
- Sit in chair
- Lift foot
- Circle ankle both directions
- Improves mobility

### Toe Spreads
- Spread toes apart
- Hold 5 seconds
- Improves toe strength and flexibility

### Calf Stretches
- Stand facing wall
- One leg back
- Keep heel down
- Lean forward
- Improves ankle flexibility

## Daily Foot Care Routine

**Morning:**
- Visual inspection  
- Put on appropriate footwear
- Check shoes for objects inside

**Evening:**
- Wash and dry feet thoroughly
- Inspect again
- Moisturize (not between toes)
- Light massage if no neuropathy

## Remember

Your feet carry you everywhere. Taking care of them and wearing proper footwear is one of the simplest fall prevention strategies.

Good shoes are an investment in safety, not a luxury. Don't compromise on footwear quality.

If you have diabetes or neuropathy, daily foot care isn't optional—it's essential for preventing serious complications and falls.

When in doubt, see a podiatrist. Foot health directly affects your independence.
