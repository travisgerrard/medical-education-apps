# Safety Tips and When to Talk with Your Doctor First

Exercise is generally safe, but there are some precautions.

## When to Check with Your Doctor
- You have heart disease or a history of heart problems
- You experience chest pain during physical activity
- You have joint problems or arthritis
- You're over 50 and haven't been active
- You're pregnant
- You have diabetes (exercise affects blood sugar)

## Start Slow
If you've been inactive, don't jump into intense workouts. Gradually build up.

## Listen to Your Body
- **Normal**: Mild muscle soreness, breathing hard but able to talk
- **Warning Signs**: Chest pain, severe shortness of breath, dizziness, joint pain

## Stay Safe
- Warm up and cool down
- Stay hydrated
- Wear appropriate footwear
- Exercise in safe, well-lit areas

> [!WARNING]
> If you experience chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or dizziness during exercise, stop immediately and seek medical attention.
