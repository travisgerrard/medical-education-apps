# Sleep, Energy, and Concentration

![Peaceful night scene with crescent moon](/images/sleep_moon.png)

Sleep problems, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating are among the most common and disruptive symptoms of both anxiety and depression. These symptoms often feed into each other, creating a challenging cycle that can be hard to break without treatment.

## Sleep Problems

Sleep disturbances affect up to 90% of people with depression and are extremely common in anxiety disorders.

**Insomnia (Trouble Sleeping)**
- Difficulty falling asleep - lying awake for 30+ minutes
- Waking up multiple times during the night
- Waking too early and being unable to fall back asleep
- Sleep that doesn't feel restful, even after enough hours

**Hypersomnia (Sleeping Too Much)**
- Sleeping 10+ hours and still feeling tired
- Difficulty getting out of bed in the morning
- Napping during the day but never feeling refreshed
- Using sleep to escape from difficult feelings

**Racing Thoughts at Night**
- Mind won't "shut off" when you try to sleep
- Replaying conversations or worrying about tomorrow
- Making mental to-do lists you can't stop
- Suddenly remembering embarrassing moments from years ago

**Nightmares and Disturbing Dreams**
- Vivid, distressing dreams that wake you up
- Dreams about threats, losses, or being chased
- Difficulty falling back asleep after a nightmare

## Energy and Fatigue

The fatigue of anxiety and depression is different from normal tiredness.

**What It Feels Like**
- Exhaustion that sleep doesn't fix
- Feeling like you're moving through molasses
- Everything takes more effort than it used to
- Needing to rest after small activities
- "Hitting a wall" partway through the day

**Why It Happens**
- Your brain is working overtime managing difficult emotions
- Chronic stress depletes neurotransmitters like dopamine and norepinephrine
- Poor sleep compounds the problem
- Reduced physical activity creates a fatigue cycle
- Depression affects your body's energy regulation

**How It Differs from Normal Tiredness**
- Doesn't improve with rest or sleep
- Present most days, not just after a busy week
- Accompanied by other symptoms (mood changes, loss of interest)
- Makes activities you used to enjoy feel exhausting

## Concentration and "Brain Fog"

Both anxiety and depression can significantly impair your ability to think clearly.

**Common Experiences**
- Difficulty focusing on tasks, especially boring ones
- Reading the same paragraph multiple times
- Walking into a room and forgetting why
- Trouble following conversations
- Making more mistakes than usual at work or school

**Memory Problems**
- Forgetting appointments or commitments
- Difficulty recalling what you did yesterday
- Searching for words that are "on the tip of your tongue"
- Forgetting names of people you know well

**Decision Fatigue**
- Simple decisions feel overwhelming
- Avoiding decisions entirely
- Second-guessing choices after making them
- Feeling paralyzed when facing options

**Slowed Thinking**
- Thoughts feel sluggish or muddled
- Taking longer to process information
- Difficulty with tasks that used to be easy
- Feeling "not as sharp" as you used to be

## The Vicious Cycle

These symptoms feed into each other:

Poor sleep leads to low energy → Low energy makes you less active → Less activity worsens depression → Depression disrupts sleep → And the cycle continues.

Breaking this cycle often requires addressing multiple areas at once.

## Helpful Strategies

**For Sleep:**
- Keep a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends
- Create a cool, dark, quiet bedroom
- Stop screens 1 hour before bed
- Avoid caffeine after noon
- If you can't sleep after 20 minutes, get up and do something boring

**For Energy:**
- Start with small amounts of physical activity
- Get morning sunlight exposure
- Take short breaks throughout the day
- Notice your natural energy patterns
- Don't rely on caffeine to compensate

**For Concentration:**
- Break tasks into smaller chunks
- Work in short focused periods (25-30 minutes)
- Reduce distractions when possible
- Write things down instead of trying to remember
- Give yourself grace - this is a symptom, not a character flaw

## When to Seek Help

Talk to a healthcare provider if:
- Sleep problems last more than 2-3 weeks
- Fatigue is severe or unexplained
- Concentration problems affect work or school
- These symptoms occur alongside low mood or excessive worry
- Self-help strategies aren't making a difference

Treatment for the underlying anxiety or depression often dramatically improves sleep, energy, and concentration.

> These symptoms are real and have biological causes - they're not laziness, weakness, or lack of willpower. Treating the underlying condition often brings significant improvement.
