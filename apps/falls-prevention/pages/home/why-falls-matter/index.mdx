# Why Falls Matter

Falls are the leading cause of injury among older adults, but understanding the impact can motivate you to take simple steps toward prevention.

## The Numbers

Every year in the United States:
- **1 in 4 adults age 65 and older** experiences a fall
- Falls send **3 million older adults** to emergency departments
- Falls lead to more than **800,000 hospitalizations** annually

But here's the good news: **most falls can be prevented**.

## Beyond Physical Injury

When someone falls, the impact goes beyond broken bones:

### Loss of Independence
- Fear of falling again may cause you to limit activities
- You might avoid social events or stop hobbies you enjoy
- Some people need help with daily tasks they used to do alone

### Emotional Impact
- Anxiety about falling can affect your confidence
- Depression may develop if you become less active
- Worry about being a burden on family members

### Financial Costs
- Emergency room visits and hospital stays are expensive
- Rehabilitation and physical therapy add up
- Home modifications or assistive devices require investment

## Falls Are NOT Normal Aging

Many people think falling is just "part of getting older." This is not true. While some changes happen with age, falls are not inevitable. With the right information and actions, you can maintain your balance, strength, and independence.

## What You Can Do

Understanding why falls matter is the first step. The rest of this program will show you specific, practical ways to reduce your risk and stay safe.

## Remember

Falls are serious, but they are preventable. Taking action now protects your health, independence, and quality of life for years to come.
