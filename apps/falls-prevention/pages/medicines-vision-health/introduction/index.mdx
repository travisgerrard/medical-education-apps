# Introduction

Beyond exercise and home safety, managing your health, vision, and medications is critical for fall prevention. This section covers medical factors you can control.

## Why Medical Management Matters

**The connection:**
- Health conditions increase fall risk
- Medications affect balance
- Vision changes make navigation harder
- Foot problems impact stability

**Good news**: Most of these factors are modifiable with proper care.

## What This Section Covers

1. Medications that increase fall risk
2. Vision and hearing health
3. Foot care and proper footwear
4. Managing dizziness and blood pressure
5. When to request a comprehensive review

## Working with Your Healthcare Team

Fall prevention requires partnership with your medical providers:
- Primary care physician
- Pharmacist
- Optometrist/ophthalmologist
- Podiatrist
- Physical therapist

## Remember

You have more control over medical fall risk factors than you might think. Regular check-ups, honest communication, and proactive management can dramatically reduce your risk.
