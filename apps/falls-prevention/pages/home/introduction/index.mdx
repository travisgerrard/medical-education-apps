# Introduction

![Staying active and confident is key to fall prevention](/images/active_seniors_hero_1763915478930.png)

Falls are one of the most common health problems facing older adults, but they are not an inevitable part of aging. Each year, millions of older adults fall, and many of these falls can be prevented.

## Why Learn About Falls?

Understanding fall prevention puts you in control of your safety and independence. Small changes in your daily routine, home environment, and health habits can significantly reduce your risk.

## What Causes Falls?

Falls rarely happen because of just one thing. Most occur when several risk factors combine:

- **Physical changes**: Weaker muscles, balance problems, or slower reflexes
- **Health conditions**: Dizziness, vision changes, or chronic illnesses
- **Medications**: Some medicines affect balance or make you drowsy
- **Environment**: Poor lighting, clutter, or slippery floors

## What You'll Learn

This program will help you:
- Understand your personal fall risk
- Make your home safer
- Build strength and balance
- Work with your healthcare team
- Know what to do if a fall happens

## Remember

Falls are predictable and preventable. By learning about your risks and taking action, you can protect your independence and stay active in the things you love.
