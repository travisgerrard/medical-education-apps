# Introduction

Exercise is a powerful, evidence-based tool for managing depression and anxiety. Physical activity has been proven to improve mood and reduce symptoms.[^1]

## What We'll Cover
- Which types of exercise help most
- How much exercise you need
- How to start when you feel unmotivated
- Safety considerations
- Staying consistent

## The Evidence
A 2024 systematic review and network meta-analysis published in the *BMJ* found that exercise interventions were 1.5 times more effective than usual care or medications for reducing symptoms of depression.[^1]

Walking, jogging, yoga, and strength training all showed significant benefits.

>
> Exercise works! Studies show it can be as effective as medication for mild to moderate depression, and it has no negative side effects.
